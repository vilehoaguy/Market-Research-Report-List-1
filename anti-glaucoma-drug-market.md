<p><strong>The Anti-Glaucoma Drug Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Miosis Drugs</li><li>Beta-Blocker</li><li>Adrenergic Agonists</li><li>Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitor</li><li>Prostaglandin Derivative Agent</li><li>Penetrates</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The anti-glaucoma drug market encompasses various types of medications aimed at reducing intraocular pressure. Miosis drugs stimulate pupil constriction to improve fluid drainage. Beta-blockers decrease aqueous humor production, thereby lowering eye pressure. Adrenergic agonists enhance fluid outflow and also reduce production. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors work by decreasing bicarbonate formation, leading to reduced fluid secretion. Prostaglandin derivative agents increase uveoscleral outflow, effectively lowering pressure. Together, these diverse classes of drugs provide multiple therapeutic options for managing glaucoma.</p></p>
