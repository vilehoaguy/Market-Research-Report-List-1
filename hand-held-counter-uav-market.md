<p><p>The hand-held counter UAV market is divided into two main types: passive recognition and active recognition. Passive recognition relies on monitoring and analyzing signals emitted by drones, identifying their presence without active interference. This approach emphasizes stealth and surveillance. In contrast, active recognition involves deliberate engagement, such as jamming signals or utilizing targeted technologies to disrupt or disable drones, allowing users to take more assertive action. Both methods play crucial roles in addressing UAV threats across various applications.</p></p>
