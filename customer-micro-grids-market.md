<p><ul><li>Grid-Tied Micro Grids</li><li>Independent Micro Grids</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Customer microgrids are categorized mainly into grid-tied and independent microgrids. Grid-tied microgrids connect to the main power grid, allowing for energy exchange and increased reliability, as they can draw power during outages. In contrast, independent microgrids operate autonomously, relying on local energy sources, making them ideal for remote areas or emergencies. These market types address different energy needs, emphasizing resilience, sustainability, and localized energy generation to enhance energy security and efficiency for consumers.</p></p>
