<p><p>The cancer vaccine market is divided into preventive and therapeutic vaccines. Preventive vaccines aim to prevent cancer from developing by targeting viruses or other factors linked to cancer, such as the HPV vaccine, which reduces cervical cancer risk. Therapeutic vaccines, on the other hand, are designed to treat existing cancer by stimulating the immune system to attack cancer cells. Each type serves distinct purposes: preventive vaccines focus on risk reduction, while therapeutic vaccines aim to enhance treatment efficacy and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
