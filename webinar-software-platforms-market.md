<p><p>Webinar software platforms operate mainly in two market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise solutions are installed locally on a user's hardware, offering greater control over data privacy and customization but requiring significant IT resources for maintenance. Conversely, cloud-based platforms run online, allowing users to access webinars from anywhere with internet connectivity. They typically offer scalability, automatic updates, and lower upfront costs, making them popular for businesses seeking flexibility and ease of use in organizing virtual events.</p></p>
