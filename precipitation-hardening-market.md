This technique involves the dissolution of soluble elements at high temperatures followed by rapid cooling, which traps these elements in a supersaturated solution. <p><ul><li>Coherency Strain Precipitation Hardening</li><li>Chemical Precipitation Hardening</li><li>Dispersion Precipitation Hardening</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Precipitation hardening is a process used to enhance the strength and hardness of materials, primarily alloys. Coherency strain precipitation hardening involves the formation of precipitates that maintain a coherent interface with the matrix, increasing strength through lattice distortion. Chemical precipitation hardening relies on specific alloying elements to create precipitates that enhance mechanical properties. Dispersion precipitation hardening integrates fine, stable particles dispersed throughout the matrix to impede dislocation movement, resulting in improved strength and fatigue resistance. Each type addresses unique material properties and applications.</p></p>
