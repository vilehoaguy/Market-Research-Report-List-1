<p><p>Modified Atmospheric Packaging (MAP) is a technology used to extend the shelf life of perishable products, primarily food, by altering the composition of gases within the packaging. By replacing the air inside the package with a controlled mixture of gases such as nitrogen, carbon dioxide, and oxygen, MAP slows down spoilage, preserves freshness, and maintains product quality. It is widely used in the food and beverage industry for products like meat, seafood, dairy, fruits, and vegetables.</p><p>The Modified Atmospheric Packaging Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period. <p><p>Modified Atmospheric Packaging (MAP) modifies the internal atmosphere of a package to extend shelf life. **Passive MAP** relies on the product's respiration to naturally alter the atmosphere, ideal for fresh produce. **Active MAP** involves injecting specific gas mixtures (e.g., CO2, N2, O2) to control spoilage and maintain freshness, commonly used for meat, seafood, and baked goods. **Others** include hybrid methods or innovations like vacuum skin packaging, combining elements of both passive and active MAP for tailored preservation solutions across diverse food products.</p></p>
